Methodology

A research framework that touches the world's wisdom traditions and aspires to inform policy decisions carries a particular obligation: every claim it makes must be one it can defend. The methodology described here is the structure we have built to meet that obligation. It is not yet a track record of findings — we have not run our pilot — but it is the discipline under which any future finding will be produced.

The Zero-Exaggeration standard

The framework operates under a Zero-Exaggeration standard. Every numeric and factual claim used in any outbound document is anchored to an entry in our Verified Facts Register — a living document that catalogues each fact's source, date of verification, and status: VERIFIED, PENDING, DERIVED, or REJECTED.

A claim moves from PENDING to VERIFIED only when a primary or strong secondary source has been logged. Claims found to be inaccurate, conflated, or unverifiable are moved to a Rejected Log, with the reasoning recorded. They are not quietly removed; they remain in the record so that future contributors can see what was rejected and why.

We do not aggregate ranges into single numbers. We do not present hypotheses as findings. We do not paraphrase scriptural or statutory text — under our Quote-Don't-Paraphrase protocol, both classes of source are quoted verbatim with their reference, or they are described qualitatively. Paraphrase introduces drift, and drift in this work is the failure mode that matters most.

The principle catalogue

Our research synthesis catalogues principles from ten wisdom traditions: Hindu, Islamic, Christian, Buddhist, Confucian, Jewish, Sikh, Taoist, Jain, and Bahá'í. Each catalogued principle is mapped to one or more specific UN Sustainable Development Goal indicators — for example, SDG 6.4 on water-use efficiency, or SDG 2.3 on the productivity of small-scale food producers.

Within the catalogue, each principle carries an explicit verification status. Where the original-language text and a specific scriptural or scholarly citation have been confirmed, the principle is flagged VERIFIED. Where the principle authentically reflects a tradition but the specific source has not yet been confirmed, the principle is flagged PENDING and is described qualitatively rather than quoted. We do not fabricate citations to fill gaps.

The verification work is itself ongoing and runs as a parallel track. Where domain scholars within a tradition agree to confirm citations, their confirmation is the standard we accept; web-search citations are treated as research leads, not as verifications.

The collaborative architecture

The framework's day-to-day research is conducted through a structured collaboration between a human principal investigator and two AI partners with distinct, transparent roles. One AI partner contributes to drafting and source-locking. The other contributes to structural design and compliance review. The human investigator holds editorial authority and integration responsibility.

We name this structure publicly because contemporary research using AI tools is most credible when it is most transparent about how those tools are used. Outputs are not generated and shipped; they are drafted, audited, reviewed, and held to the same Zero-Exaggeration standard described above. Where an AI partner produces text that fails an audit — whether through fabrication, paraphrase drift, or misattribution — the failure is logged in our Rejected Log alongside any other rejected claim. The discipline applies regardless of source.

Verification gates

The pilot's progression is gated, not assumed.

Gate 1 — Baseline Lock. Before any intervention, a region-specific baseline is established for the indicators the pilot will measure. Items currently flagged PENDING in the Verified Facts Register are sourced and locked at this gate, or the work does not proceed past it.

Gate 2 — Field Data Milestone. After ninety days of pilot activity, the first quarter of validated field data is generated and reviewed. This gate is also the trigger for any wider engagement with institutional partners — the work transitions from proposal to evidence here.

Gate 3 — Independent Audit. At project end, the dataset is reviewed by an independent fiscal and social audit. Any effect of the pilot is computed at this gate from observed deltas, not from any prior expectation. A null finding is a valid research outcome; the methodology does not presume an effect.

This methodology will be revised as the work matures. Its current form reflects the discipline we have committed to maintain through the project's pre-pilot stage; subsequent revisions will be logged transparently, with each version anchored to the version of the Verified Facts Register on which it depends.

We make the methodology public because the framework's integrity ultimately rests not on its findings but on whether its findings can be trusted. The discipline above is the structure under which that trust is built.
